The Real Problem

Bangladesh's Fake Parts Crisis Is Costing Bikers Dearly

Every day, thousands of motorcycles across Bangladesh slow down, overheat, or break down — not because of road conditions, not because of the rider's mistake, but because of one silent killer: নকল বা নিম্নমানের যন্ত্রাংশ (fake or substandard spare parts).

Walk into any roadside parts shop in a small town and you'll likely find shelves full of look-alike components — engine parts, fuel tanks, brake assemblies, LED lights — all designed to fool the eye but not built to last. The packaging mimics the original. The price feels like a deal. But within weeks, sometimes days, the performance begins to deteriorate, and the cost of fixing the damage far outweighs what you "saved."

For riders of Bajaj Pulsar, Yamaha FZ, TVS Apache, or any popular bike in Bangladesh, this is not a hypothetical risk. It is a daily reality. And it's why finding a genuinely trustworthy spare parts supplier has become one of the most important decisions a motorcycle owner can make.

  • Counterfeit engine parts reduce engine life by up to 60%
  • Fake brake pads and discs dramatically increase stopping distance — a real safety hazard
  • Low-quality fuel filters cause injector damage costing thousands of taka to repair
  • Imitation LED lights and electricals short-circuit, risking fire
  • Substandard body panels crack and fade within one monsoon season
  • No warranty, no accountability — local shops often vanish after a complaint

The question isn't whether fake parts are a problem in Bangladesh — it's: where do you go to guarantee you're getting the real thing?
